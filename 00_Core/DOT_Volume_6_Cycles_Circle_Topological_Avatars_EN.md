# 1. Task of the Topological Reading

The strict rank-3 scene is finite. It has six states and finite
relations.

Topology enters as an avatar: it reads a finite cyclic or axial structure
as a continuous object.

The main discipline of this volume is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{topological objects are readings of the finite core.}
}
\]

The finite core is primary for DOT; the topological language shows how the
same structure can be projected into continuous geometry.

---

# 2. Finite Source

The source is the first complete scene:

\[
S_3=(U_3;R_1,R_2,R_3).
\]

Its three relation layers are:

\[
R_1=C_6,
\]

\[
R_2=K_3\sqcup K_3,
\]

\[
R_3=3K_2.
\]

Volume 6 focuses mainly on \(R_1\) and \(R_3\), while \(R_2\) enters as
the triadic chamber layer.

---

# 3. Cycle \(C_6\)

Choose the cycle

\[
100\to110\to010\to011\to001\to101\to100.
\]

Let \(T\) be one step along this cycle.

Then

\[
\boxed{
T^6=\operatorname{id}.
}
\]

The half-period is

\[
\boxed{
T^3=\kappa.
}
\]

Thus a half-turn of the cycle sends each state to its complement.

---

# 4. From Cycle to Circle

The finite cycle \(C_6\) can be placed on the circle \(S^1\) as six
equally spaced points.

This gives a continuous avatar:

\[
C_6\leadsto S^1.
\]

The finite step \(T\) becomes rotation by

\[
\frac{2\pi}{6}.
\]

The half-period \(T^3\) becomes rotation by

\[
\pi.
\]

This is the continuous form of complementarity.

---

# 5. Roots of Unity

Let

\[
\zeta_6=e^{2\pi i/6}.
\]

Then the six states of the cycle can be represented by

\[
1,\zeta_6,\zeta_6^2,\zeta_6^3,\zeta_6^4,\zeta_6^5.
\]

The cyclic step is multiplication by \(\zeta_6\).

The half-period is

\[
\zeta_6^3=e^{\pi i}=-1.
\]

Thus

\[
T^3=\kappa
\]

has the continuous avatar

\[
e^{\pi i}=-1.
\]

This is a bridge reading, not a replacement of the finite law.

---

# 6. Half-Period as Möbius Type

A full cycle returns to the same state:

\[
T^6=\operatorname{id}.
\]

A half-cycle changes side:

\[
T^3=\kappa.
\]

This gives a Möbius-type reading: after half a traversal, the side is
reversed; after a full traversal, the initial state is restored.

The strict statement remains finite:

\[
T^3=\kappa,\qquad T^6=\operatorname{id}.
\]

---

# 7. Axial Pairs

The polar layer is

\[
R_3=3K_2.
\]

It consists of three axes:

\[
\{100,011\},
\]

\[
\{010,101\},
\]

\[
\{001,110\}.
\]

Each axis is a pair of opposite states.

---

# 8. One Axis and Hopf Reading

Topologically, one polar pair can be read as a pair of linked circles:

\[
K_2\leadsto\text{Hopf pair}.
\]

This is an avatar of polar inseparability: the two sides are distinct but
belong to one linked structure.

This reading is external to the strict graph. The strict layer is still
the two-point relation \(K_2\).

---

# 9. Three Hopf Pairs

Since

\[
R_3=3K_2,
\]

the topological avatar is three Hopf pairs:

\[
H_1,\qquad H_2,\qquad H_3.
\]

Each \(H_i\) reads one complementary axis as a linked pair.

Together they form a six-component topological package.

---

# 10. Triadic Layer \(R_2\)

The triadic layer is

\[
R_2=K_3\sqcup K_3.
\]

It contains two closed triads:

\[
S_1^{(3)},\qquad S_2^{(3)}.
\]

In topological reading, these two triads organize the three axial pairs
into two complementary chamber systems.

---

# 11. Borromean Triad of Blocks

The three Hopf pairs can be read as three blocks:

\[
H_1,H_2,H_3.
\]

The Borromean avatar reads these three blocks as linked together in such
a way that the triple is held as a whole.

Symbolically:

\[
\operatorname{Bor}(H_1,H_2,H_3).
\]

This is the topological analogue of the threefold holding of the first
complete scene.

---

# 12. Borromean Linkage of Holding Conditions

In the finite core, the three holding conditions appear through:

\[
R_3=3K_2,
\]

\[
R_2=K_3\sqcup K_3,
\]

\[
R_1=C_6.
\]

The Borromean topological reading says: the complete scene is held by the
triple structure. Removing one layer changes the scene.

The topological link is an avatar of this finite structural dependence.

# 14. Möbius, Hopf, and Borromean Readings

The three main topological avatars are:

- Möbius type: side change under half-period;
- Hopf type: linked pair as avatar of one axis;
- Borromean type: three linked blocks as avatar of triple holding.

They correspond to different parts of the finite scene:

\[
T^3=\kappa,
\]

\[

\[

# 15. Circle and Boundary

The circle \(S^1\) is a continuous boundary without endpoints.

The finite cycle \(C_6\) is the first strict cyclic boundary of the DOT
active scene.

Thus the passage

\[
C_6\leadsto S^1
\]

is the bridge from finite cyclic closure to continuous closed boundary.

---

# 16. From \(C_6\) to Higher Cycles

Higher ranks can contain many cycles. A single carrier can support
different cyclic atlases.

The cyclic reading of \(U_3\) is special because \(R_1\) itself is a
single \(C_6\).

In higher ranks, cycles are no longer automatically the whole
one-distance relation. They must be selected or organized as an atlas.

---

# 17. Boundary with Constructive Arithmetic

The cyclic avatars connect to constructible polygons through roots of
unity.

The Fermat-prime line

\[
3,5,17,257,65537
\]

belongs to the bridge between finite DOT carriers, cyclic avatars, and
classical constructibility.

The rank thresholds associated with these primes are:

\[
3,\ 5,\ 9,\ 17,\ 33.
\]

This is an arithmetic horizon, not part of the strict rank-3 proof.
