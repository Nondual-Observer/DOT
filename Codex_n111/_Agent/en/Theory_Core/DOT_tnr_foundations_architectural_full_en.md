# Preamble: The Canonical Method of Projections

## P.1. Methodological Fixation

The entire theory is formulated **strictly from within itself**.

This means:

### P.1.1. Absence of an External Position

There is no "view from outside." Any statement is a **projection** from an already operative structure of distinction.

### P.1.2. Mandatory Level Indication

Every statement must indicate the **level** at which it is admissible:

| Level | Content | What is defined |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **0** | Invariants (prohibitions) | Only impossibilities |
| **1** | Permissions (modes) | Admissible configurations |
| **2** | Axes and limits | Stabilised directions |
| **3** | Nodes and body | Architecture of projections |

### P.1.3. Separation of Levels

Prohibitions, permissions, axes, and signs **are not mixed across levels**.

What at one level is a **prohibition** may manifest at another as a **limit** or as a **direction** — but this is *a different projection*, not "the same thing."

### P.1.4. Refusal to Describe "Reality as Such"

No statement describes "reality as such."

A statement always describes:
- either the **impossibility of a position of utterance**;
- or the **admissibility of a mode**;
- or the **architecture of sustaining distinction**.

> **This preamble is not a meta-description of the theory and is itself subject to the stated method of projections.**

---

## P.2. Requirements for a Canonical Statement

Any canonical statement within the theory **must contain**:

1. **Projection level** (0, 1, 2, or 3)
2. **Type of prohibition** (position, mode, act)
3. **Architectural correlation** (to which future axis/mode it pertains)

> **Without these data a statement cannot be interpreted within the canon of the theory and is deemed inadmissible for further projection.**

# §0. Foundations of the Theory: Invariants (Level 0)

## §0.1. Status of the Section

This section fixes **level zero** of the theory of observable distinction.

### §0.1.1. What "Level Zero" Means

By level zero is understood the level **preceding**:

- the introduction of objects;
- the introduction of axes;
- the introduction of geometric or topological forms;
- the introduction of dynamics, time, and processes;
- the introduction of signs, quantities, and metrics.

### §0.1.2. Character of Level-0 Statements

All statements in this section are formulated **exclusively** as statements about *the impossibility of certain positions of distinction*.

Any positive articulation (definition, object, direction, structure) at this level is considered **methodologically inadmissible**.

### §0.1.3. What §0 Does NOT Contain

This section **does not contain**:

| Absent | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| Axioms in the classical sense | Axioms assert existence |
| Definitions of objects | Objects arise later |
| Laws or principles | Laws require a domain of applicability |
| Constructive procedures | Procedures require operations |

The section contains **only invariant prohibitions** fixing the limits of admissibility of distinction as such.

### §0.2.1. Definition

By **position of distinction** is understood the minimal logical context in which it is possible to assert the presence of a difference.

### §0.2.2. What a Position of Distinction Is NOT

A position of distinction:

| Is NOT | Reason |

| An object | Requires distinctions of levels 1–3 |

A position of distinction is **the condition of articulability of difference**, but is not itself articulated positively.

### §0.2.3. To What Do Level-0 Prohibitions Pertain

All level-0 prohibitions pertain:
- **NOT** to the results of distinction

## §0.6. Primary Invariants (Prohibitions Z)

This section fixes the **primary invariants of the theory** in the form of prohibitions. These prohibitions:

- are not statements about the world, objects, or states;
- set the **boundaries of admissible logical positions of distinction**;
- reside at a level preceding any architecture.

### §0.6.0. Methodological Status of the Prohibitions

All level-0 prohibitions possess the following **mandatory properties**:

| Property | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Projection level** | Level 0 — before the introduction of axes, objects, states, processes |
| **Type of prohibition** | Prohibition of a logical position of distinction, not of a state or object |

| **Architectural status** | Condition of possibility of the future architecture, but not its foundation and not its element |

> **Level-0 prohibitions fix not *what is* but *which ways of asserting distinction are logically inadmissible*.**

> *Note: Sub-items §0.6.X.1–§0.6.X.7 of each prohibition contain canonical commentaries and are not part of the formal prohibition. Formal prohibition = formulation + type + reduction + correlation.*

### §0.6.1. Zᴰ — Prohibition of Coincidence

**Future projection:** Axis Distinction (D) — not introduced at this level.

#### Canonical Formulation (Level 0)

> ❌ **Impossible is a position of distinction at level 0 in which the distinguishing and the distinguished coincide.**

---

#### §0.6.1.1. Level and Type of the Prohibition

| Aspect | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Projection level** | Level 0 (before architecture, before axes, before objects) |
| **Type of prohibition** | Prohibition of a logical position of distinction |
| **IS NOT** | A prohibition of a state, a prohibition of an object, a prohibition of ontological identity |

Prohibition Zᴰ **does not assert** the impossibility of coincidence as such.

It asserts the impossibility of *simultaneously* fulfilling two requirements:
1. asserting the presence of distinction;
2. occupying a position in which difference is absent.

**It is precisely this combination that is logically inadmissible.**

---

#### §0.6.1.2. What Is Meant by "Position of Distinction"

At level 0, by a position of distinction is understood:

> The minimal logical context in which it is possible to assert that an act of distinction takes place at all.

This is:
- **NOT** a subject
- **NOT** a point of observation
- **NOT** a system state
- **NOT** an object

It is the position from which an utterance of the form *"distinction is effected"* is admissible.

Prohibition Zᴰ pertains **exclusively** to this position and does not extend to any subsequent ontological constructions.

---

#### §0.6.1.3. Why This Is NOT a Prohibition of a State

A state presupposes:
- stability;
- sustaining;
- the possibility of re-identification.

All these characteristics appear **only after** the introduction of fixation (future axis F).

At level 0 the concept of state **is not yet lawful**.

**Consequently:**
- the theory does not prohibit the state of identity;
- the theory does not assert that a system cannot be in a state of coincidence;
- the theory merely asserts that **from a state of coincidence it is impossible to occupy a position of distinction**.

---

#### §0.6.1.4. Why This Is NOT a Prohibition of an Object

Objecthood presupposes:
- separability;
- identifiability;
- a boundary.

All these characteristics are **derivatives** of distinction and cannot be used for its primary prohibition.

Prohibition Zᴰ **does not assert**:
- that an object cannot coincide with itself;
- that the distinguishing and the distinguished cannot be one object.

It asserts that **objecthood cannot serve as a foundation for distinction** if difference is not given from the outset.

---

#### §0.6.1.5. The Prohibited Reduction

Prohibition Zᴰ eliminates the following reduction:

> **The reduction of distinction to a nominal designation without difference.**

Formally this means:
- the act of distinction is declared;
- difference is not effected;
- the distinguishing and the distinguished coincide;
- distinction loses its functional load.

**Result:** Distinction collapses into identity and ceases to be distinction.

---

#### §0.6.1.6. Disappearance of the Distance of Difference

By distance here is understood **logical distance**, not spatial or metric.

**Logical distance** is the minimal irreducibility of the distinguishing and the distinguished.

If this distance equals zero:
- the directionality of distinction is absent;
- the possibility of indicating "from where" and "towards what" is absent;
- distinction is indistinguishable from its absence.

**It is precisely this situation that is prohibited.**

---

#### §0.6.1.7. Atomic Commentary

| Layer | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Position of distinction** | Minimal context of distinction |
| **Act of distinction** | The very fact of introducing difference |
| **Prohibited** | Collapse of distinction into identity |
| **Implicitly present** | Sustaining, Autonomy |

**📌 Meaning:** Distinction cannot have zero distance.

---

#### §0.6.1.8. Consequences of the Prohibition (Level 0)

From prohibition Zᴰ at level 0 it immediately follows:

1. **The impossibility of total identity** as a position of distinction.
2. **The impossibility of zero logical distance** when asserting distinction.
3. **The impossibility of the position "inside without difference"**, since the concept of "inside" already presupposes a boundary and difference.

These consequences are not additional postulates; they are **logically equivalent** to the prohibition itself upon the unfolding of its content.

---

#### §0.6.1.9. Architectural Correlation

Prohibition Zᴰ is **the condition of possibility** for the stabilisation of the future axis of distinction D.

It:
- **does not introduce** axis D;
- **does not define** its structure;
- **does not specify** its directionality.

> **Axis D arises later as the architectural institutionalisation of the impossibility of coincidence, fixed by prohibition Zᴰ.**

### §0.6.2. Zᶠ — Prohibition of Tracelessness

**Future projection:** Axis Fixation (F) — not introduced at this level.

#### Canonical Formulation (Level 0)

❌ Impossible is a position of distinction at level 0 in which the act of distinction leaves no distinguishable trace.

---

#### §0.6.2.1. Level and Type of the Prohibition

Type of prohibition:
- prohibition of a logical position of distinction;
- not a prohibition of an object;
- not a prohibition of a state;
- not a prohibition of a process or event.

Prohibition $Z_F$ is formulated before the introduction of:
- time;
- duration;
- dynamics;
- form.

Consequently, it:
- does not prohibit disappearance in time;
- does not prohibit instantaneity as a process;
- does not appeal to memory as a mechanism.

What is prohibited is a position in which distinction is logically indistinguishable from its absence.

In other words: if distinction leaves no trace, then there is no position from which one can assert that distinction has taken place.

---

#### §0.6.2.2. The Prohibited Reduction

The sole prohibited reduction:

> **The reduction of distinction to an act without distinguishability.**

In this reduction:
- distinction is effected;
- but it leaves not a single structural sign of its having been effected;
- and therefore cannot be fixed as distinction.

Such an act:
- cannot be repeated;
- cannot be recognised;
- cannot be included in the architecture of distinction.

Consequently, it is equivalent to the absence of distinction.

**It is precisely this equivalence that is prohibited.**

---

#### §0.6.2.3. Why This Is NOT a Prohibition of a State

If $Z_F$ were a prohibition of a state, distinction could occur but be in an "unresolved state."

This is impossible because:
- at level 0 there are no states;
- there is only the admissibility or inadmissibility of a position of distinction.

Prohibition $Z_F$ fixes:

> if distinction leaves no trace, the position of distinction is impossible in principle.

Not "prohibited," not "unstable," but impossible.

---

#### §0.6.2.4. Immediate Consequences

From $Z_F$ it immediately follows:

1. **Pure instantaneity of distinction is impossible.**
Distinction cannot be of logically zero duration if that zero-ness destroys the distinguishability of the fact of distinction.

2. **An act without a trace is impossible.**
Any admissible act of distinction must leave a minimal structural residue.

3. **The position "distinguished, but never was" is impossible.**
A position in which the completedness of distinction is asserted without the possibility of its recognition is logically contradictory.

4. **The reduction of distinction to pure potentiality is impossible.**
Potentiality without a trace is indistinguishable from zero.

---

#### §0.6.2.5. Implicit Connections with Other Prohibitions

Although $Z_F$ is formulated autonomously, it is implicitly connected with:
- $Z_D$ — without distance there is nothing to sustain;
- $Z_C$ — without autonomy the trace would be externally imposed.

However:
- $Z_F$ is not derivable from either of them;
- its removal destroys distinction even when the others are preserved.

This fixes the Borromean status of the prohibition.

---

#### §0.6.2.6. Architectural Correlation

Prohibition $Z_F$ is the negative condition of possibility for the future axis of fixation $F$.

Important:
- $Z_F$ does not introduce memory;
- $Z_F$ does not introduce time;
- $Z_F$ does not introduce sustaining as a mechanism.

It merely fixes:

> without a trace, distinction is structurally impossible.

Axis $F$ appears only at the architectural level as a stabilised direction permitted by this prohibition.

| Criterion | Status |

### §0.6.3. Zᶜ — Prohibition of External Closure

**Future projection:** Axis Consistency (C) — not introduced at this level.

#### Canonical Formulation (Level 0)

❌ Impossible is a position of distinction at level 0 in which distinction requires an external measure, an external centre, or an external completion.

#### §0.6.3.1. Level and Type of the Prohibition

Type of prohibition:
- prohibition of a logical position of distinction;
- not a prohibition of an object;
- not a prohibition of a state;
- not a prohibition of a system;
- not a prohibition of a law.

Prohibition $Z_C$ operates before the appearance of:
- systems;
- rules;
- global scales;
- metalanguages;
- external observers.

It pertains exclusively to the admissibility of the position from which distinction can be effected at all.

The prohibition does not assert that external measures are impossible as such; it asserts that distinction cannot depend on them in its foundation.

---

#### §0.6.3.2. The Prohibited Reduction

$Z_C$ prohibits the reduction of distinction to a form in which:

1. **The completedness of distinction is determined from outside.**
Distinction is deemed to have occurred only upon appeal to an external criterion.

2. **The centre of coordination is placed beyond the limit of distinction.**
Distinction requires a point not belonging to the distinction itself.

3. **The measure of distinguishability is given transcendently.**
Difference is evaluated relative to a universal scale not arising from the distinction itself.

Each of these reductions eliminates the autonomy of distinction as a minimal act.

---

#### §0.6.3.3. Why This Is NOT a Prohibition of a State

- a state presupposes an already existing system of differences;
- a system presupposes coordination;
- coordination presupposes an internal principle of sustaining.

$Z_C$ operates earlier than all these levels.

It prohibits the attempt to occupy a position of distinction in which:
- distinction has not yet occurred;
- but its possibility has already been made dependent on an external foundation.

Thus:
- $Z_C$ does not prohibit states of coordination;
- $Z_C$ prohibits the dependence of the possibility of distinction on external coordination.

---

#### §0.6.3.4. Immediate Consequences

From $Z_C$ it immediately follows:

1. **A global measure of distinguishability is impossible.**
No scale can be universally applied to all distinctions at level 0.

2. **An external observer as a foundation of distinction is impossible.**
Any appeal to a "view from outside" destroys the position of distinction.

3. **The reduction of distinction to a rule is impossible.**
A rule can arise only after distinction, but cannot determine its possibility.

4. **Transcendent completedness is impossible.**
Distinction cannot be completed by reference to something outside itself.

---

#### §0.6.3.5. Implicit Connections with Other Prohibitions

- Without $Z_D$ the external centre would coincide with the distinguished and yield a collapse into identity.
- Without $Z_F$ the external measure would leave no internal trace and yield a collapse into disappearance.
- Without $Z_C$ distinction loses its autonomy and turns into a function of a meta-order.

Consequently, $Z_C$:
- is not derivable from $Z_D$;
- is not derivable from $Z_F$;
- is necessary for sustaining the self-sufficiency of distinction.

This confirms its Borromean status.

---

#### §0.6.3.6. Architectural Correlation

$Z_C$ is the condition of possibility for the future axis of consistency $C$.

Important:
- axis $C$ is not embedded in $Z_C$;
- $Z_C$ merely prohibits forms of distinction in which consistency is given from outside;
- consistency itself appears only at the architectural level.

# §1. Legalization: The Mechanics of Permissions (Level 1)

## §1.0. Methodological Position

### §1.0.1. Connection with §0

§1 is formulated **strictly within the canon of §0** and does not introduce new foundations.

### §1.0.1a. Prohibition of Direct Transition

A direct transition from level-0 prohibitions to architectures, axes, or structures is **inadmissible**.

Prohibited:
- treating prohibitions as constraints on objects or states;
- introducing positive configurations as given;
- interpreting prohibitions as conditions of existence.

### §1.0.1b. The Sole Admissible Operation

Only the following operation is admissible:

> Consideration of the **joint sustaining** of the invariant prohibitions as a condition of admissibility of modes of distinction.

### §1.0.2. What §1 Does NOT Use

All definitions in §1:
- do not appeal to objects, quantities, axes, or directions;
- do not use the signs +/−;
- do not presuppose motion, time, or dynamics;
- are formulated exclusively as **admissible modes of distinction**.

### §1.0.3. The Fundamental Difference between R and Z

> **Permissions (R) are not the opposites of Prohibitions (Z).**

- A permission **is not** "the absence of a prohibition."
- A permission is **a configuration that survives under the action of the prohibitions**.

> *A configuration at level 1 possesses no internal relations, order, or metric and denotes exclusively the joint sustaining of the invariant prohibitions.*

§1.0.4.1. General Setup

This sub-item does not introduce new prohibitions, permissions, or architectural elements.

Characteristics:

The position of distinction is the minimal logical position in which it is admissible to assert that distinction takes place.

It is to this position that:
	•all level-0 prohibitions pertain;
	•all level-1 permissions pertain.

The position of distinction:
	•is not an act;
	•is not a process;
	•is not a state;
	•is not an object.

It is the logical status of the admissibility of difference.

The permissions of §1 do not describe the distinguished;

## §4.3. Vertices: Poles and Liminal Projections

The octahedron has **6 vertices** corresponding to the poles of the axes:

$$
\pm D, \quad \pm F, \quad \pm C
$$

### §4.3.1. Status of the Vertices

Vertices are:

* **liminal projections** of the axes;
* **not** field states;
* **not** stable modes.

### §4.3.2. The Two-Faced Character of a Vertex

Each vertex is fundamentally **two-faced**:

| Side | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **+** | Limit of the realisation of distinction |
| **−** | Limit of the reduction of distinction |

A vertex:

* does not belong to any face in its entirety;
* does not sustain the structure;
* is the point of maximal tension and therefore of maximal instability.

> **Vertices delimit but do not sustain the architecture.**

Consequences:

* flows do not pass through vertices;
* no constants are localised at vertices;
* vertices serve only as markers of limits.

## §4.4. Edges: Transition and Tension

The edges of the octahedron are a **fundamental yet transitional element** of the architecture.

### §4.4.1. Canonical Definition

> **Edges are the minimal admissible transitions between liminal projections, in which two axes are active and the third is sustained as an invariant.**

### §4.4.2. Properties of Edges

| Are not trajectories | — |
| Are **bearers of tension** | ✓ |

It is on the edges that:

* distinction has not yet collapsed;
* but is not yet stabilised in a face.

### §4.4.3. Barycentres of Edges

Barycentres of edges:

* do not coincide with vertices;
* do not lie at the centre;
* are **unstable points of direction**.

They introduce for the first time:

* orientation;
* vectoriality;
* the possibility of a field.

> **Barycentres of edges are the bridge between the architecture (§4) and fields (§5).**

The octahedron has **8 faces**:

### §4.5.2. Consequences

### §4.6.3. General Status of Liminal Faces

+++ and ---:

> **They fix the limits but do not participate in the architecture of sustaining.**
