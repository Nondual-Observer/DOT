## 2. The Building Atom Is Not Minimal but Closed

The primary building atom of TNR cannot be read as:

- a point;
- an edge;
- an angle;
- a minimal segment.

The more accurate statement is:

> the building atom is ternary not in the sense of “minimal three,” but in the
> mode of **closure**.

So what is primary is not “three elements by themselves,” but a
**closed ternary knot**.

This means:

- point is a reduction;
- edge is a reduction;
- face is a reduction;
- intersection is a reduction;

and their source is one and the same:

**the act of closed ternary connectedness.**

This fits well with:

- Borromean logic;
- the secondary status of duality;
- the ban on simple coincidence;
- the impossibility of a “simple point” as a full ontological primitive.

### D. `witness`

### F. Closure as translation from event into law

In this branch closure cannot be understood only as a
“closed contour” or “finished figure.”

More exactly:

**closure** is the transition in which a manifested state ceases to be only a
local event and begins to act as a stable law of the next regime.

So closure has at least three readings:

1. **local**
   - a figure closes;
   - a node becomes operable;
2. **interval**
   - the manifested node starts to define the period-law of the next segment;
3. **architectural**
   - an already assembled structure collapses into the node of the next level.

In short:

> closure does not only complete a form;  
> closure translates manifestation into an invariant mode of action.

### G. Classes of closure

At the current stage it is reasonable to distinguish at least four classes.

#### 1. Local closure

A manifested structure becomes locally operable:

- the figure is held;
- the node is assembled;
- the object is available for action.

#### 2. Interval closure

A manifested node stops being only an event and begins to set the interval-law:

- cadence;
- periodicity;
- repeated regime;
- invariant of the interval.

#### 3. Self-lock closure

A node or mode is caught by its own repetition or by the repetition of its
structural companion:

- `q^2` as a prime self-lock;
- `4 = 2+2 = 2*2` as the special binary self-lock.

#### 4. Architectural closure

An already assembled scene or structure is no longer just locally stabilized,
but becomes the building atom of the next level.

### H. Transitions between closure classes

#### 1. `local -> interval`

The node stops being only a local event and begins to govern cadence.

#### 2. `interval -> self-lock`

Periodicity stops being mere recurrence and becomes self-support.

#### 3. `self-lock -> architectural`

Self-support becomes the building atom of the next level.

#### 4. Reverse reading

At a new scale an architectural node can again be read as local closure.

So:

> closure does not merely move upward;  
> closure rewrites its role when scale changes.

## 2.2. The Threshold Ladder: `0 -> NOT -> 1 -> 2`

This branch eventually had to move earlier than `2×2`.

It became clear that the minimal threshold ladder is:

`0 -> NOT -> 1 -> 2`

and that everything later rests on it.

### A. `0` as the limit without carrier

`0` here is not a positive thing and not an empty object.

It is the limit where no retained carrier has yet appeared.

### B. `NOT` as the carrying processual boundary

`NOT` is not merely a logical negation.

It is the carrying processual boundary that:

- prevents coincidence;
- keeps distinction from collapsing;
- carries the first articulation.

### C. Interval `0 -> 1`: the first dominance of additivity

From `0` to `1` the additive reading dominates.

The reason is simple:

- additivity reaches the first local limit earlier;
- multiplicative articulation has not yet manifested its own lock.

### D. Interval `1 -> 2`: the second dominance of additivity

The same remains true from `1` to `2`.

The additive line still dominates because the multiplicative branch has not yet
received its own explicit point of manifestation.

### E. What to do with negative reading

Negative reading here should not be treated as a ready ontology of “negative
objects.”

It is better held as:

- shadow reading;
- reverse sign;
- or a prohibited return through the same limit.

### F. Node `2` as the first orthogonal birth of the multiplicative branch

Node `2` is the place where the multiplicative branch first becomes visible,
but not as a new thing arising from nowhere.

It appears at an intersection of already present primitive laws.

Its first explicit lock is:

`2+2 = 2*2 = 4`

So before `2` multiplicativity is hidden; at `2` it first receives a readable
point of its own.

### G. The law of dominance

The dominant flow is the one that reaches the local limit first.

This law explains:

- the early dominance of additivity;
- the later manifestation of multiplicative closure;
- and the asymmetry of many later branches.

### H. Why this matters

Without this early ladder later structures become arbitrary symbols.

With it they become readable as a sequence of thresholds.

### I. The inter-node boundary as the field of primitive dominance

The boundary between nodes is not an empty gap.

It is the processual layer where primitive laws compete for dominance.

### J. Processuality and object as two sides of one articulation

The result of that competition is accumulated in nodes.

So action and quantity, processuality and objecthood, are two sides of one and
the same articulation.
