## 1. The Pre-Primitive Ground: Fullness and Zero

### 1.1. Fullness (unity without local distinction)

The theory does not start from emptiness.

> **Fullness** is unity without local distinction: undifferentiated fullness.

That is:

- not “there is nothing”;
- but “everything has not yet been cut”;
- what is absent is not the carrier, but local distinction inside the carrier.

Fullness is the **Pre-Primitive Ground**.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

In set theory, the analogue of Fullness is the universal class $V$, which contains everything but is not itself a set. In topology, it is an undivided connected space prior to the introduction of open coverings. Fullness precedes any formal system, because a formal system already presupposes distinction between signs.

### 1.2. Zero (the first non-local state of readout)

> **Zero** is the first readout of fullness in the absence of local distinction.

$$
0 + 0 = 0, \qquad 0 \cdot 0 = 0
$$

Both operations create no new entity. They simply register that fullness, taken with itself, remains projectively undifferentiated.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In Peano arithmetic, $0$ is the initial element and is not the successor of anything.
- In ring theory, $0$ is the additive identity and the multiplicative annihilator.
- In TNR, $0$ is not empty, but **non-local**: it is the readable state of fullness that has not yet been articulated.

### 1.3. The relation Fullness → Zero

- Fullness precedes projective reading.
- Zero is the first state of that reading.
- But Zero is not yet a local state.

## 2. Primitive Operators: Not → Cut → Support → Witness → Knot

### 2.1. Not (law of non-coincidence)

> **Not** is the law that forbids the coincidence of the distinct.

Not:

- preserves distinction;
- prevents fullness and manifestation from collapsing into each other;
- establishes the very possibility of future distinction.

Its relation to Zero: Not is the first operator acting **inside** Zero.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In logic: the law of identity ($A=A$) and the law of non-contradiction ($\neg(A \wedge \neg A)$).
- In Spencer-Brown’s theory: the primary distinction (mark / distinction) in *Laws of Form*.
- In topology: the separation of a connected space into components requires a prior prohibition of point-coincidence.

### 2.2. Cut (the first local articulation of the prohibition)

> **Cut** is the first local articulation of Not.

- Not without Cut is possible as a global law.
- Cut without Not is impossible.

> Not is more primary than Cut; Cut is the localization of Not.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In topology: a Dedekind cut, a splitting of linear order into two classes.
- In sheaf theory: a section is a local choice of value from a global structure.
- In TNR: Cut turns distinction from “forbidden in general” into “forbidden here.”

### 2.3. Support (retained extension)

> **Support** is retained extension between already distinguished poles.

- Not preserves non-coincidence itself.
- Cut localizes that non-coincidence.
- Support is what first retains localized non-coincidence as extension.

The minimal condition of Support is not “many cuts,” but a **retained cut**: an ongoing Not continues to hold the Cut, and thus the first state-of-between appears.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In set theory: the interval $(a,b)$ presupposes elements between two distinguished points.
- In topology: the support of a function is the closure of the set where it is nonzero.
- In TNR: Support is not yet a ready-made line, but **extension held by the prohibition**.

### 2.4. Witness (order of passage and reading)

> **Witness** is the order of passage and reading.

It makes possible:

- time;
- causality;
- a path of traversal;
- local meaning.

Order of emergence: first Support, then Witness turns retained extension into a readable path.

Two modes:

- **Passive Witness** — the order of readout that serializes extension.
- **Active Witness** — the witness that participates in stabilizing closure.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In order theory: linear (total) order is an antisymmetric, transitive, total relation.
- In model theory: a Henkin witness is an element confirming an existential claim.
- In computation: an execution trace is a sequence of steps.
- In TNR: Witness gives **direction of reading** to extension, thereby creating a path.

### 2.5. Closed Ternary Knot (the first closure)

> **Closed Ternary Knot** is the first closure-binding whole.

- Support gives retained extension.
- Witness gives the path and readout of that extension.
- Closed Ternary Knot is what first turns that path into **a whole that returns into itself**.

This means:

- the point is not primary;
- the edge is not primary;
- the face is not primary;
- **primary is closed ternary connectedness**.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In graph theory: a cycle of length 3 is the minimal nontrivial cycle.
- In algebraic topology: a 1-cycle in a simplicial complex is a closed 1-chain with zero boundary.
- In knot theory: the trefoil is the simplest nontrivial knot.
- In category theory: a commutative triangle is the minimal nontrivial diagram.
- In TNR: Knot shows that **the three operators (Cut, Support, Witness) cannot coexist in an open state** — they close.

## 3. The Threshold Ladder: from 0 to 2

$$
\text{Fullness} \to 0 \to \text{NOT} \to 1 \to 2
$$

### 3.1. 1 as First Seal

> **1** is the First Seal — the first seal of local manifestation.

Arithmetically:

- $1 \cdot 1 = 1$ — preserves identity;
- $1 / 1 = 1$ — self-identical readout;
- $1 + 1 = 2$ — the additive branch passes through 1 but does not stop there.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In ring theory: $1$ is the multiplicative unit.
- In category theory: the terminal object is an object into which there is a unique morphism from every other object.
- In TNR: 1 is the **Seal**, not yet the node of branching. It is the act of local hermetic fixation.

### 3.2. 2 as First Threshold-Node

> **2** is the first threshold-node where the hidden branch gets a chance to become manifest.

- As a local fixation, 2 is already a node.
- As a transition to the next regime, 2 is still a threshold.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In Peano arithmetic: $S(S(0)) = 2$, the first successor of a successor.
- In binary systems: the minimal basis of nontrivial branching.
- In TNR: $2$ is the **first fork**, the first formation of a pair.

### 3.3. The law of additive dominance up to 2

> The dominant flow is the one that reaches the local threshold sooner.

On the intervals $0 \to 1$ and $1 \to 2$, additive reading dominates:

$$
0 + 1 = 1,\quad 0 \cdot 1 = 0

$$
1 + 1 = 2,\quad 1 \cdot 1 = 1
$$

Up to node 2, it is addition that reaches the local threshold first.

The multiplicative branch manifests for the first time only at the intersection of already active primitive laws. Its first full sign is:

$$
2 + 2 = 2 \cdot 2 = 4

## 4. The Small Structural Basis: $1 \to 2 \to 3 \to 5 \to 6 \to 11$

### 4.1. $4 = 2+2 = 2 \cdot 2$ — First Self-Lock

For $4$, additive and multiplicative readings coincide. This is the only such node:

$$
q + q = q^2 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad q = 2
$$

Therefore, $4$ reads as:

- the self-lock of symmetry;
- a quadrature node;
- the first stable symmetry enclosure.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In linear algebra: a $2 \times 2$ matrix is the minimal nontrivial square matrix.
- In group theory: the Klein group $V_4 = \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2$ is the minimal noncyclic group.
- In TNR: $4$ is the only point where **the additive and multiplicative self-lock coincide**.

### 4.2. $6 = 1+2+3 = 1 \cdot 2 \cdot 3$ — First Manifest-Carrier

Here additive and multiplicative readouts of the small basis coincide:

$$
\text{seal}(1) + \text{cut}(2) + \text{knot}(3) = 6
$$
$$
\text{seal}(1) \cdot \text{cut}(2) \cdot \text{knot}(3) = 6
$$

Therefore $6$ is the first node where both branches close into one machine. It is:

- the first manifest-carrier;
- the first small machine-node;
- the first operable machine of the small basis.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In TNR: $6$ is the **first harmonic coincidence** of sum and product on one and the same basis.

### 4.3. $5 = 2+3 \neq 2 \cdot 3 = 6$ — Bridge-Invariant

$5$ matters because it does **not** coincide with the carrier-machine:

2 + 3 = 5, \qquad 2 \cdot 3 = 6

The connection is already there, but it has not yet collapsed into an explicit carrier. Therefore $5$ is:

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In TNR: $5$ is **additive connectedness without multiplicative closure**.

### 4.4. $11 = 1+2+3+5 = 6+5$ — Total Readable Whole

$$
11 = \underbrace{1 + 2 + 3}_{= 6 \text{ (machine)}} + 5 \text{ (bridge)}

$11$ yields not merely a machine, but a **fully readable assembly**:

- $6$ — the explicit machine;

**Relation to classical foundations:**

## 5. Additive-Multiplicative Resonance

### 5.1. Two flows

Additive and multiplicative readings are not two branches of arithmetic, but **two projections of one structure**:

```text
additive flow:        1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → ... → p
multiplicative flow:  2 · 3 · 5 · 7 · ... · p
```

- **Additive flow** (Additive Hull, $A(p)$): continuity of passage, witness-order, sequence, closure through adjacency.
- **Multiplicative flow** (Multiplicative Scaffold, $M(p)$): structural weaving, scaffold, carrier-field, architectural articulation.
- **Prime numbers**: discrete invariants that survive both readings.

In short:

> the additive side answers **how the structure passes**;  
> the multiplicative side answers **how the structure is assembled**;  
> the primes answer **what remains irreducible under both readings**.

### 5.2. Additive Hull $A(p)$

$$
A(p) = \sum_{n=1}^{p} n = \frac{p(p+1)}{2}
$$

$A(p)$ is the full additive envelope of the natural flow up to the horizon $p$.

Example for horizon $p = 5$:

$$
A(5) = 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 = 15
$$

### 5.3. Multiplicative Scaffold $M(p)$

$$
M(p) = \prod_{\substack{q \le p \\ q \in \mathbb{P}}} q = p\#
$$

$M(p)$ is the primorial up to horizon $p$.

Example for horizon $p = 5$:

$$
M(5) = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 = 30
$$

The number $4 = 2^2$ does not enter here, because it is a derived self-lock structure rather than a new primitive.

### 5.4. Envelope $A(p) + M(p)$: the case $p = 5$

$$
15 + 30 = 45
$$

$$
\frac{15}{45} = \frac{1}{3}, \qquad \frac{30}{45} = \frac{2}{3}
$$

This yields a very clean reading:

- $15$ — closure-side with weight $1/3$;
- $30$ — articulation-side with weight $2/3$;
- $45$ — the Envelope within which both sides appear as shares of one whole.

> The additive and multiplicative states are unequal in load, yet symmetric in origin, and distributed inside one whole as $1/3$ and $2/3$.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In combinatorics: triangular numbers $T_n = n(n+1)/2$.
- In number theory: the primorial $p\#$ is a basic object of analytic number theory.
- In TNR: the weights $1/3$ and $2/3$ are a form of **stable asymmetric complementarity** (closure vs articulation), independent of external choice.

### 5.5. Half-Step Companion $h = \frac{p+1}{2}$

For an odd prime horizon $p$:

$$
A(p) = p \cdot h, \qquad h = \frac{p+1}{2}
$$

Then:

$$
\frac{M(p)}{A(p)} = \frac{p\#}{p \cdot h} = \frac{(p-1)\#}{h}
$$

This is the main law of the corridor:

> The Additive Hull acts as a **cancellation mask** on the Multiplicative Scaffold.

- $p$ cancels immediately;
- what remains is the lower prime-field;
- the companion $h = (p+1)/2$ cuts out its own modes from that field;
- the residue shows what survives after additive cancellation.

### 5.6. Squarefree Criterion: integer vs fractional tail

The primorial $(p-1)\#$ carries each prime factor **only once**.

Hence:

- if $h$ is **squarefree**, additive cancellation proceeds cleanly, and the residue is **integer**;
- if $h$ contains a repeated prime factor, a **fractional tail** appears.

> Integer residue ↔ squarefree companion;  
> fractional tail ↔ repeated multiplicity in the companion.

Examples:

| Horizon $p$ | $h = \frac{p+1}{2}$ | Squarefree? | $\frac{M(p)}{A(p)}$ | Type |
|---:|---:|---|---|---|
| $5$ | $3$ | Yes | $2$ | Integer |
| $7$ | $4 = 2^2$ | No | $15/2$ | Fractional tail |
| $11$ | $6 = 2 \cdot 3$ | Yes | $\frac{2310}{66} = 35$ | Integer |
| $13$ | $7$ | Yes | $\frac{30030}{91} = 330$ | Integer |

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In number theory: squarefree integers are a fundamental notion of multiplicative number theory. The Möbius function satisfies $\mu(n)=0$ for non-squarefree integers.
- In TNR: the squarefree criterion separates **clean passage** from **passage with debt**.

$$
1 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot \ldots \cdot p

Then:

$$
N(p) := 1 + \pi(p)
$$

where:

- $1$ is anchor / seal / support, the first support-node;
- $\pi(p)$ is the number of primes not exceeding $p$.

### 6.2. The triplet of horizon invariants

(A(p),\; M(p),\; N(p))

- $A(p)$ — **additive continuity**: how far passage has gone;

- $N(p)$ — **scaffold dimension**: how many active “participants” are in the package.

$$
2 + 2 \quad \longleftrightarrow \quad 2 \cdot 2

- two binary lines;

$2 \cdot 2$ means:

- quadrature;
- the first symmetry-lock;
- the minimal articulated frame.

> $2+2$ gives the **scene** of coordination;

This is precisely where symmetry / asymmetry, reflection, reciprocity, and reciprocal ratios naturally belong.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In linear algebra: $\mathrm{GL}(2,\mathbb{R})$ is the group of nondegenerate $2 \times 2$ matrices, the minimal algebra of transformations.

- In TNR: $2 \times 2$ is the **minimal grammar of mutual coordination**.

### 7.3. Observer-Cut (the minimal logic of the observer)

After $2 \times 2$, Witness receives a new regime:

- reflective witness;
- mutually reversible witness;
- mutual witness of articulated sides.

Only after this does the following become possible:

> **Observer-Cut** — the derived asymmetry of an already activated regime of mutual witnessing.

Order:

1. $2 \times 2$ gives a bilaterally articulated scene;
2. both sides can mutually witness one another;
3. one side is selected as the **support of readout**, the other as the **observed**.

> Observer-Cut is the minimal **Observer Logic**.

**Relation to classical foundations:**

- In quantum mechanics: the measurement problem, where the choice of projection basis collapses a superposition.
- In category theory: choosing a terminal morphism fixes one of the projections of a product.
- In TNR: Observer-Cut is **not an external observer**, but an **internal asymmetry** of an already existing bilateral scene.
