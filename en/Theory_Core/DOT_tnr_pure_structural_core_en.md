## 1. Articulation

The key general term of this branch:

> **Articulation** —
> this is the transition of hidden connectivity
> into a manifest, distinguishable, and operable form.

This definition is deliberately broader
than the words:

- process;
- action;
- object;
- form;
- meaning.

The reason is simple:

- at the boundary one and the same structure
  appears as unfolding;
- at the node it appears as object;
- in sustaining it appears as meaning.

Therefore it is better to say:

> Articulation
> is the general term for that which
> in one projection is read as process,
> in another as object,
> and in a third as meaning.

From this three derived terms immediately follow.

### 1.1. Articulation Field

**Articulation field** —
this is the inter-nodal layer
where primitive-laws have not yet collapsed into an object
but contend for manifest dominance.

This is:

- a zone of tension;
- a field of redistribution;
- a layer of competition;
- not yet a finished object.

### 1.2. Articulation Node

**Articulation node** —
this is the place
where the result of the articulation field
collapses into a locally operable form.

That is, the node:

- is not simply a point;
- is not simply a trace;
- but an accumulator of the result
  achieved in the inter-nodal layer.

### 1.3. Held Articulation

**Held articulation** —
this is articulation
that no longer flows as an event
but is sustained as a whole.

In human reading
this is precisely how the following arise:

- meaning;
- readable form;
- coherent object of understanding.

In brief:

> Articulation
> at the boundary is process;
> at the node is object;
> in sustaining is meaning.

### 1.4. Projection Apparatus

One more general term is needed,
so as not to confuse
mathematical language,
dimensionality,
and the regime of manifestation itself.

> **Projection Apparatus** —
> this is the dimensional
> and formal regime
> within which
> hidden connectivity
> becomes readable as a projection.

This is more important
than simply saying
"mathematical apparatus",
because in DOT
we do not simply record an object,
but choose the regime
in which it can be seen at all.

In brief:

> we do not create an object out of the void;
> we create a projection,
> using the projection apparatus,
> and already within it
> fullness begins to be read
> as a distinguishable form.

## 2. Primitive Laws

In DOT the early foundation
cannot be read through Euclidean primitives:

- point;
- line;
- face.

Primary are not geometric elements,
but **Primitive Laws**.

### 2.0. Fullness

The early start of DOT
cannot be read
from empty space.

More precisely:

> what is originally given is not void,
> but **Fullness** —
> unity without local distinction,
> undifferentiated fullness.

That is:

- not "nothing exists";
- but "everything is not yet cut";
- the carrier is not absent;
- local distinction within the carrier is absent.

This is the key shift:

- we do not create an object ex nihilo;
- we create a projection;
- and only within a projection
  does something begin to be read as an object.

#### 2.0.A. Status of Fullness

This point can now
be closed firmly.

`Fullness`
should not be considered
the first `Primitive Law`.

More precisely:

> `Fullness`
> is **Pre-Primitive Ground**,
> or ground of projection,
> from which primitive-laws
> only begin to articulate themselves.

The reason is simple:

- a law
  already presupposes
  some regime of distinction;
- `Fullness`
  does not yet distinguish,
  but only provides the fullness
  within which distinction will become possible;
- consequently,
  `Fullness`
  precedes the primitive-laws
  but does not belong to them
  as an equal element.

In brief:

> `Fullness`
> is not the first law,
> but the pre-primitive ground
> of all subsequent articulation.

### 2.1. Zero

**Zero** —
is not an "empty number" in the school sense,
but:

- the first readout of fullness
  in the absence of local distinction;
- the limit of undifferentiated fullness;
- pre-objectual non-manifestation;
- fullness without local cut.

Therefore:

$$
0 + 0 = 0,
\qquad
0 * 0 = 0
$$

do not create a new entity.

They only fix
that fullness,
taken with itself,
remains projectionally undifferentiated.

#### 2.1.A. Status of Zero

Here too a more precise
formula is needed.

`Zero`
should not be considered
the first `local state`.

But it is also too weak
to say
that it is "merely a sign".

More precisely:

> `Zero`
> is the **First Nonlocal Readout-State**.

This means:

- `Zero`
  already belongs
  to the `Projection Apparatus`,
  and does not stand entirely outside it;
- but `Zero`
  does not yet localise difference
  and therefore is not
  the first local state;
- `Zero`
  fixes the state of fullness
  in the regime of absence of local cut.

That is:

> `Fullness`
> precedes projectional reading;
> `Zero`
> is the first state of that reading,
> but not yet the first local state.

Or more rigorously:

> `Zero`
> is the first projectional state,
> but not the first local state.

### 2.2. Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)

**Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)** —
this is not merely a logical particle.

This is:

- a bearing processual boundary;
- a law of sustaining of difference;
- that which does not allow
  undifferentiated fullness
  and local manifestation
  to coincide directly.

In the early core of DOT
it is precisely `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
that should be considered
the first genuine bearing function.

Because without it:

- there is no distinction;
- there is no first boundary;
- there is no transition from `0` to `1`;
- there is no future articulation.

### 2.3. Cut

**Cut** —
the first local act of distinction.

If `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
is a law,
then `Cut` —
is already its local action.

It yields:

- "this / other";
- a local boundary;
- binary distinction;
- the first admissible discreteness.

#### 2.3.A. Relation of Not and Cut

This point requires a rigorous separation.

`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
and `Cut`
should not be considered:

- either fully identical;
- or two independent primitive-laws
  of the same order.

More precisely:

> `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
> is the deeper law of non-coincidence;
> `Cut`
> is its localisation
> and first operable manifestation.

That is:

- `Not`
  belongs to the early boundary-law;
- `Cut`
  already belongs to local articulation.

#### 2.3.B. What Not Can Do That Cut Does Not Yet Do

`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)` already:

- sustains difference;
- does not allow fullness and manifestation
  to collapse directly;
- sets the very possibility of future distinction.

But by itself it does not yet yield:

- a local line;
- local addressing;
- a concrete "this / other";
- a finished binary node.

That is:

> `Not`
> is not yet a localised law of difference.

#### 2.3.C. What Cut Does

`Cut` takes the next step.

It:

- localises `Not`;
- translates the law
  into a concrete distinction-event;
- creates the first local discreteness;
- makes possible
  binary articulation.

That is:

> `Cut`
> is `Not`
> become a locally readable act.

#### 2.3.D. Asymmetry of This Pair

From this an important asymmetry follows:

- `Not` without `Cut`
  is possible as a deep law;
- `Cut` without `Not`
  is impossible.

Because:

- the law of difference
  may not yet be localised;
- but a local cut
  cannot arise
  without an already operative prohibition of coincidence.

In brief:

> `Not` is more primary than `Cut`;
> `Cut` is the localisation of `Not`.

#### 2.3.E. Consequence for the Early Ladder

This immediately clarifies the ladder:

$$
\text{Fullness} \to 0 \to NOT \to 1 \to 2
$$

Here:

- `Not`
  stands before local binary articulation;
- `Cut`
  manifests
  when the law of non-coincidence
  must already be realised
  in local distinction.

Therefore:

> from `0` to `1`
> what works is primarily `Not`;
> from `1` to `2`
> `Not` already begins to read itself
> as `Cut`.

#### 2.3.F. Summary Formula

The briefest working formula here is:

> **Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)** —
> is the deep law of difference;
> **Cut** —
> is the localised act of that law.

Or more rigorously:

> `Not` is a law;
> `Cut` is the first local articulation of that law.

### 2.4. Support

**Support** —
is not a ready-made line,
but the sustained extension
between already distinguished poles.

From `Support` are subsequently read:

- line;
- length;
- order;
- sequence;
- admissible extension.

#### 2.4.A. From Not to Support

After the introduction of `Fullness`
this connection is already read much more rigorously.

Here it is important not to confuse
three different regimes of one and the same movement:

- `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
  sustains non-coincidence itself;
- `Cut`
  localises that non-coincidence;
- `Support`
  for the first time sustains
  localised non-coincidence
  as extension.

That is:

> `Not`
> yields the impossibility of direct coincidence;
> `Cut`
> yields the local boundary of that impossibility;
> `Support`
> yields the first retained extension
> of that local boundary.

This means
that `Support`
cannot be read as
yet another independent primitive
of the same type as `Not` and `Cut`.

More precisely:

> `Support`
> is the first regime
> in which difference
> is not only sustained,
> but also stretches.

This is precisely why:

- line is not primary;
- length is not primary;
- sequence is not primary;
- primary is the sustained support
  between already separated poles.

#### 2.4.B. The First Between

If stated as briefly as possible,
`Support`
is the first genuine
**between-state**.

`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
does not yet yield "between";
it only prohibits direct collapse.

`Cut`
yields a boundary,
but does not yet sustain extension
between the poles.

And only `Support`:

- sustains distance;
- makes a path possible;
- creates the first carrier of extension.

That is:

> from `Not` to `Cut`
> distinguishability is born;
> from `Cut` to `Support`
> extension is born.

#### 2.4.C. Retained Cut

Here the following
precise question arises:

> is one `Cut` or several
> needed for `Support`?

The working formula now reads:

> `Support`
> is born not from a simple multiplicity of cut-events,
> but from a **retained cut**.

This means:

- one raw `Cut`
  yields only a local boundary;
- one retained `Cut`
  already yields two stably readable poles;
- and precisely between them
  the first support arises.

Therefore:

- `Support`
  does not necessarily require
  several independent cut-relations;
- but it does not arise either
  from a momentary cut-event
  that has not yet been retained.

In brief:

> not "many cuts",
> but a **retained cut**
> is the minimal condition
> for the emergence of support.

Multiple cut-relations
appear only later,
when from the minimal support
the following begin to assemble:

- network;
- graph;
- scene;
- higher articulation.

#### 2.4.D. What Retains the Cut

Here it is important not to fall into a circle.

One cannot say
that `Support`
retains the `Cut`,
if `Support` itself
arises only from it.

Equally it is too early to say
that`Witness`
retains the `Cut`,
because in the current ladder
`Witness`
appears later as the order of readout,
not as the minimal condition
of the first extension.

The working formula now is:

> `retained cut`
> is retained by the continuing action of
> `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
> on one and the same region of
> `Fullness`.

That is:

- `Fullness`
  yields the undifferentiated substrate;
- `Not`
  sustains the impossibility of collapse;
- `Cut`
  localises that impossibility;
- and when `Not`
  continues to act
  on an already localised difference,
  `Support` arises.

In brief:

> `Support`
> is not "memory of a cut-event",
> but the local form of how
> `Not`
> continues to sustain difference
> after its localisation.

Hence the more rigorous formula:

> `retained cut`
> is `Cut`
> not released by `Not`.

### 2.5. Witness

**Witness** —
this is the order of traversal and the order of readout.

It is precisely what makes possible:

- time;
- causality;
- scan-path;
- local meaning.

#### 2.5.A. Witness Comes After Support

After the separation of `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`,
`Cut`,
and `Support`,
this point too can be made more rigorous.

`Witness`
is not needed
to produce the first support.

Because:

- `Support`
  already yields the between-state;
- `Support`
  already sustains extension;
- but `Support`
  does not yet set
  a definite order of traversal.

Therefore:

> first `Support` arises;
> then `Witness`
> transforms sustained extension
> into a readable path.

#### 2.5.B. Passive Witness and Active Witness

Here it is useful to distinguish
two regimes.

**Passive Witness** —
this is readout-order
that simply serialises extension.

**Active Witness** —
this is witness
that already participates
in the stabilisation of closure.

That is:

- at the level of `Support`
  witness is still passive;
- at the level of closure
  witness becomes active.

#### 2.5.C. Witness Activation at Closure

The minimal step
where `Witness`
ceases to be
merely the order of reading
is the `Closed Ternary Knot`.

Because it is precisely there that:

- the path does not merely pass;
- the path returns;
- and the return becomes
  part of the structure itself.

In brief:

> `Support`
> yields extension;
> `Witness`
> yields the path;
> `Closed Ternary Knot`
> makes that path closure-binding.

### 2.6. Closed Ternary Knot

**Closed Ternary Knot** —
the first complete constructive act
in which connectivity already sustains itself as a whole.

This means:

- point is not yet primary;
- edge is not yet primary;
- face is not yet primary;
- primary is the closed ternary connectivity.

#### 2.6.A. From Support and Witness to Knot

After the preceding steps
this connection is already read quite stably:

- `Support`
  yields retained extension;
- `Witness`
  yields the path/readout of that extension;
- `Closed Ternary Knot`
  for the first time makes the path
  return into a whole.

That is:

> `Support`
> sustains the between;
> `Witness`
> conducts the path through the between;
> `Knot`
> binds the path
> with return.

#### 2.6.B. Closure-Binding Whole

This allows a more precise definition to be given.

`Closed Ternary Knot`
is not merely "three connections"
and not merely "a closed figure".

More precisely:

> this is the first
> **closure-binding whole**,
> in which the path
> no longer merely passes through
> but also returns
> as part of the structure itself.

This is precisely why
for the first time the following become compatible here:

- extension;
- readout;
- return;
- whole.

#### 2.6.C. Knot Before `2×2`

Here it is important not to mix levels.

`Closed Ternary Knot`
is not yet `2×2 Logical Projection`.

The difference is:

- `Knot`
  yields the first closure-whole;
- `2×2`
  yields the later scene of mutual articulation.

That is:

> `Knot`
> closes the movement into a whole;
> `2×2`
> already unfolds the whole
> into a symmetrical scene of coordination.

This is precisely why
`Knot`
must be understood earlier
than we proceed
to the reflective and reciprocal logic of `2×2`.

## 3. Threshold Ladder

The earliest structural ladder
that can now be held rigorously:

$$
\text{Fullness} \to 0 \to NOT \to 1 \to 2
$$

This is not merely a sequence of values.

This is the first corridor,
readable within
the Projection Apparatus,
in which the following are visible:

- filled fullness;
- zero readout;
- prohibition;
- first seal;
- and the first branching threshold.

### 3.1. Interval `0 -> 1`

On the interval `0 -> 1`
**Additive Reading** dominates.

This is visible as follows:

$$
0 + 1 = 1,
\qquad
0 * 1 = 0,
\qquad
\frac{0}{1}=0.
$$

That is:

- additive passage
  already reaches the first seal;
- multiplicative reading
  does not yet have its own branch
  and returns the system
  to zero-readout.

Hence the first law:

> up to `1`
> additivity dominates,
> because it is the first
> to reach local manifestation.

#### 3.1.A. Unit `1` as First Seal

After the new start
the status of `1`
is better read not as a full node,
but as:

- first seal;
- first identity-fixation;
- first locally held manifestation.

Why precisely so:

- `1 * 1 = 1`
  sustains identity,
  not an unfolded branch-logic;
- `1 / 1 = 1`
  shows a self-coinciding readout,
  not a new splitting;
- `1 + 1 = 2`
  means
  that the additive branch
  passes through `1`
  but does not stop at it
  as a full binary node.

Therefore:

> `1`
> is the first seal of local manifestation,
> but not yet a full branching node.

Or more rigorously:

> `1`
> is the first seal,
> not the final node.

### 3.2. Interval `1 -> 2`

On the next interval
additivity still dominates:

$$
1 + 1 = 2,
\qquad
1 * 1 = 1,
\qquad
\frac{1}{1}=1.
$$

That is:

- additive reading
  reaches a new node;
- multiplicative reading
  still sustains only identity.

Hence the second law:

> up to node `2`
> additivity remains the dominant regime.

### 3.3. The Role of Negative Mark

The expression

$$
0 - 1 = -1
$$

should not yet be read
as an already autonomous negative ontology.

At the current level
`Negative Mark`
is better understood as:

- shadow-sign;
- boundary debt;
- the trace of unreachable manifestation;
- but not as an already assembled object of another type.

That is, the minus here
is better read as a mark of prohibition,
not as a new independent branch.

## 4. Dominance Law

From the early ladder
a simple
but very powerful law follows:

> **Dominance Law**:
> the flow that reaches
> the local limit first dominates.

This means:

- on `0 -> 1`
  the additive branch reaches the limit first;
- on `1 -> 2`
  the additive branch again arrives at the node earlier;
- only at `2`
  does the first possibility arise
  for another branch
  to obtain its own point of collapse.

Consequently:

> dominance is determined
> not by the "strength" of the flow,
> but by the speed of reaching the local limit.

Node `2`
cannot be read as simply "the next number".

This is:

- the first binary node;
- the first stable bifurcation point;
- the first threshold
  where the hidden branch
  gets a chance to become manifest.

#### 5.0.A. Node or Threshold?

After the separation of the early steps
this question can already be closed.

The correct formula here is:

> `2`
> is the **first threshold-node**.

This means:

- internally `2`

- relative to the next layer
  `2`
  remains a threshold
  to `2×2` articulation.

That is:

> as a local fixation
> `2`
> is already a node;
> as a transition to the next regime
> `2`
> is still a threshold.

This is precisely why
one cannot choose
between "node" and "threshold"
as between two mutually exclusive options.

`2`
is both,
but in different readings.

### 5.1. Why the Multiplicative Branch Does Not Arise from Nothing

In DOT a new entity
does not appear ex nihilo.

More precisely:

> a new branch manifests
> at the intersection of already operative primitive-laws.

This is precisely why node `2`
is so important:

- before it, multiplicative reading is hidden;
- in it, for the first time it receives
  the condition of independent manifestation;
- but this manifestation is born
  not separately from the additive branch,
  but on the same binary material.

### 5.2. First Multiplicative Sign

The first complete sign of this shift:

$$
2 + 2 = 2 * 2 = 4.
$$

This means:

- additive repetition
  still continues the scene;
- but already on the same material
  a structural lock arises;
- multiplicative articulation
  for the first time manifests as closure,
  not as hidden possibility.

That is:

> `4`
> is the first explicit sign
> that the multiplicative branch has become visible.

## 6. `2×2` Logical Projection

After node `2`
the next layer arises:

$$
2 + 2
\quad\longleftrightarrow\quad
2 * 2
$$

This is no longer an ordinary `2D`,
but a **Logical Projection** of coordination.

### 6.1. `2+2` as Bilateral Scene

`2+2`
here means:

- two binary lines;
- two conjugate sides;
- minimal reflective field.

This is the first scene
where the structure can be:

- symmetrical;
- reflective;
- mutually correlated.

### 6.2. `2*2` as Structural Lock

`2*2`
means:

- quadrature;
- first symmetry-lock;
- minimal articulated frame.

Hence:

> `2+2`
> yields the scene of coordination,
> and `2*2`
> yields its lock.

### 6.3. What Belongs to `2×2`

It is precisely here that the following naturally reside:

- symmetry / asymmetry;
- reflection;
- reciprocity;
- reciprocal ratios;
- lock / scene pairs.

Later, precisely from here the following grow:

- `1/3 \leftrightarrow 3`
- `1/2 \leftrightarrow 2`
- `2/3 \leftrightarrow 3/2`

But this is already the next step,
not the earliest primitive-layer.

### 6.4. Witness Beyond Knot

This point can now be closed,
rather than held as an indeterminacy.

`Closed Ternary Knot`
is sufficient for the **primary complete activation**
of `Witness`.

That is, it is precisely in the `Knot` that:

- `Witness`
  ceases to be merely readout-order;
- `Witness`
  becomes closure-binding;
- the path for the first time returns into a whole.

But `2×2`
does not simply repeat the same thing.

`2×2`
yields a **secondary witness-regime**:

- reflective witness;
- reciprocal witness;
- mutual witness of articulated sides.

That is:

> `Knot`
> yields the first complete activation of witness;
> `2×2`
> yields the higher reflective regime of witness.

This is precisely why:

- without `Knot`
  there is not yet a complete closure-bound witness;
- without `2×2`
  there is not yet a reflective mutual witness.

### 6.5. Observer-Cut after `2×2`

Now one more step can be taken
without mixing it with the primitives.

`Observer-Cut`
is not:

- `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`;
- `Cut` of the early core;
- or simply any `Witness`.

More precisely:

> `Observer-Cut`
> is a derived asymmetry
> of the already activated
> mutual witness-regime in `2×2`.

That is:

- first `2×2`
  yields a bilaterally articulated scene;
- then both sides
  can mutually witness each other;
- and only after this
  can one side be
  chosen as readout-support,
  and the other —
  as the observed side.

It is precisely this directional choice
that constitutes `Observer-Cut`.

#### 6.5.A. Observer Logic

From this it follows
that `Observer Logic`
is not primary.

It arises only when
the following already exist:

- an articulated bilateral scene;
- mutual witness;
- the possibility of asymmetrical fixing
  of one side as support.

That is:

> observer-logic
> is not the beginning of distinction,
> but the directional stabilisation
> of an already mutually articulated scene.

#### 6.5.B. Minimal Observer Formula

The minimal formula now is:

> `Knot`
> yields closure-bound witness;
> `2×2`
> yields reflective mutual witness;
> `Observer-Cut`
> yields directional witness.

Or more rigorously:

> the observer
> does not produce witness;
> the observer
> chooses the side
> of an already activated witness-regime.

#### 6.5.C. Minimal Observer Logic

This question can now be closed.

`Observer-Cut`
is the minimal
`Observer Logic`.

Why:

- before `2×2`
  there is only mutual witness;
- in `Observer-Cut`
  for the first time
  directional asymmetry of reading appears;
- it is precisely this asymmetry
  that yields the minimal difference
  between observer-side
  and observed-side.

That is:

> observer-logic
> begins not where
> witness exists at all,
> but where
> witness becomes directional.

#### 6.5.D. Directional Stabilisation Comes Later

But `Observer-Cut`
does not exhaust
all later observer-layers.

After it, the following are still possible:

- more stable regimes of fixing of the side;
- repeated stabilisation of perspective;
- role-packaging of directional witness;
- later observer-architectures.

That is:

> `Observer-Cut`
> is the minimal observer-logic;
> `directional stabilisation`
> is already its further superstructure.

### 6.6. Boundary Between `2×2` and Role-Geometry

This point can now
be separated rigorously.

The early `2×2 Logical Projection`
is not yet
`Role Geometry`.

The reason is
that `2×2`
works as:

- bilateral scene;
- reflective pair;
- mutual articulation;
- minimal lock between sides.

That is, here
the structure is still read
as relation-grammar
between two sides
of one coordinated scene.

The later `Role Geometry`
begins only where

## 10. Strong Laws (of the Early Core)

At the current stage
the following strong laws can already be held.

### 10.1. Law of Fullness-First Start

The early core of DOT
begins not from void,
but from `Fullness`.

`Fullness`
should be considered
not the first primitive-law,
but `Pre-Primitive Ground`
of the chosen projection basis.

`Zero`
should be read
not as emptiness
and not as the first local state,
but as the first nonlocal readout-state
of that fullness
in the chosen projection apparatus.

### 10.2. Law of Prohibitive Support

`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)` —
is not an empty logical symbol,
but a bearing boundary,
without which the first manifestation is impossible.

### 10.3. Law of Not/Cut Asymmetry

`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
and `Cut`
are not identical.

The correct hierarchy is:

- `Not`
  is the deeper law of non-coincidence;
- `Cut`
  is the first local articulation of that law.

Hence:

- `Not` without `Cut`
  is possible as a boundary-law;
- `Cut` without `Not`
  is impossible.

### 10.4. Law of Support Emergence

`Support`
is not an independent
third entity of the same order
as `Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`
and `Cut`.

More precisely:

- `Not`
  sustains non-coincidence;
- `Cut`
  localises it;
- `Support`
  sustains that localisation
  as extension.

That is:

> `Support`
> is the first retained extension
> of localised difference.

Or more rigorously:

> the minimal condition for the emergence of `Support`
> is not a multiplicity of cut-relations,
> but a **retained cut**.

### 10.5. Law of Retained-Cut Minimality

A single momentary `Cut`
yields only a boundary.

`Support` arises when
`Cut`
does not vanish as an event
but is retained
as a pair of readable poles.

Hence:

- for minimal support
  a single retained cut is sufficient;
- multiple cut-relations
  already belong
  to the next level of network articulation.

### 10.6. Law of Not-Retention

`retained cut`
is retained not by the future `Support`
and not by the later `Witness`,
but by the continuing action of
`Not (Prohibition of Coincidence)`.

Hence:

- `Fullness`
  yields the substrate;
- `Not`
  sustains non-coincidence;
- `Cut`
  localises it;
- `Support`
  is the first local form
  of that sustaining.

### 10.7. Law of Unit-Seal Status

`1`
should not be read
as an already full node.

More precisely:

- `1`
  is the first seal of local manifestation;
- `1`
  sustains identity;
- `1`
  does not yet yield full binary branching.

This is precisely why:

- `1 * 1 = 1`
  sustains the seal;
- `1 / 1 = 1`
  sustains self-readout;
- `1 + 1 = 2`
  shows
  that the additive passage
  proceeds further
  to the first branching threshold.

### 10.8. Law of Witness Posteriority to Support

`Witness`
does not produce the first support.

The correct order is:

- first `Support`
  yields retained extension;
- then `Witness`
  serialises that extension
  as path/readout.

That is:

> witness does not precede support,
> but follows it.

### 10.9. Law of Witness Activation at Closure

`Witness`
becomes structurally active
not on the extension itself,
but in closure.

The minimal step of this is the
`Closed Ternary Knot`.

While only `Support` exists,
`Witness`
still remains readout-order.

When the path
begins to return into a whole,
`Witness`
becomes closure-binding.

### 10.10. Law of Knot as First Closure-Binding Whole

`Closed Ternary Knot`
is the first step
where the following are simultaneously assembled:

- retained extension;
- readout-path;
- return;
- whole.

That is:

> `Knot`
> is the first closure-binding whole.

### 10.11. Law of Additive Dominance before `2`

Up to node `2`
additive reading
remains the dominant regime,
because it is the first
to reach the local limit.

### 10.12. Law of Threshold-Node Status of `2`

`2`
should not be read
either as "only a node"
or as "only a threshold".

More precisely:

- `2`
  is the first binary node
  as a local fixation;
- `2`
  is a threshold
  as a transition
  to the next regime of articulation.

That is:

> `2`
> is the first threshold-node.

### 10.13. Law of Branching at `2`

Node `2`
is the first threshold
where the multiplicative branch
becomes capable
of an independent manifest lock.

### 10.14. Law of `2×2` Articulation

`2+2`
yields the scene of coordination,
and `2*2`
yields its structural lock.

### 10.15. Law of Secondary Witness Regime at `2×2`

`Closed Ternary Knot`
is sufficient for the
first complete activation of
`Witness`
as closure-binding.

But `2×2`
yields not the same,
but a higher witness-function:

- reflective;
- reciprocal;
- mutual between articulated sides.

That is:

> `Knot`
> activates witness as closure-binding;
> `2×2`
> transfers witness
> into the reflective mutual regime.

### 10.16. Law of Observer-Cut as Derived Asymmetry

`Observer-Cut`
does not belong
to the earliest primitive-laws.

It arises only after
the mutual witness-regime
has already been activated
in `2×2`.

That is:

- first there is reflective mutual witness;
- then one side
  is fixed as readout-support;
- the other side
  becomes the observed side.

It is precisely this directional choice
that is `Observer-Cut`.

### 10.17. Law of Observer-Cut as Minimal Observer Logic

`Observer-Cut`
is already sufficient
for the emergence of
minimal observer-logic.

Because it is precisely here that:

- one side
  is fixed as support;

  as the observed side.

That is:

> observer-logic
> begins with the directional asymmetry of witness.

### 10.18. Law of Boundary/Node Duality

Boundary field
and node
are not two different entities,
but two projections of one articulation:

- at the boundary it flows;
- at the node it is accumulated.

### 10.19. Law of Quantity/Action Unity

Quantity and action
are not opposed.

They are two sides of one and the same movement:

- one side shows unfolding;
- the other —
  the accumulated readout of that unfolding.

### 10.20. Law of Projectional Reading

The early core

ontology of "things in themselves".

within the `Projection Apparatus`.

Hence:

- `Fullness`

That is:

> `2×2`
